# 10 Coaching Business Templates

Copy any prompt. Paste into Qwen Code. Get a complete system back.

All of these work 10x better because you built your QWEN.md in Lesson 1. Qwen knows your business, your clients, and your voice.

## 1. Content Calendar Generator

```
Create a 4-week content calendar for my coaching business. Include:
- 3 posts per week for LinkedIn
- 2 Instagram posts per week with caption ideas
- 1 email newsletter per week
- Topics that build toward my offer
- Mix of educational, personal, and promotional content

Format as a table with: Week, Platform, Topic, Content Type, CTA.
```

---

## 2. Discovery Call Script

```
Write a complete discovery call script for my coaching business. Include:
- Opening rapport-building questions (5 minutes)
- Deep-dive discovery questions (15 minutes)
- Gap analysis framework (where they are vs. where they want to be)
- How to present my programme as the solution (without being salesy)
- Close with clear next steps

Include exact wording I can use, written in my voice.
```

---

## 3. Client Welcome Email Sequence

```
Create a 3-email welcome sequence for new coaching clients.

Email 1 (immediately after signup): Welcome, what to expect, how to prepare
Email 2 (2 days before first session): Homework, mindset prep, logistics
Email 3 (day of first session): Reminder, excitement, link to join

Make each email warm, professional, and actionable. Include subject lines.
```

---

## 4. Sales Page Generator

```
Write a complete sales page for my coaching programme. Include:
- Headline that speaks to my ideal client's pain
- Problem section (agitate the pain)
- Solution section (introduce my programme)
- What's included (modules, support, bonuses)
- Transformation section (before/after)
- Testimonials section (use placeholders I can fill)
- FAQ section (handle objections)
- CTA section with urgency (without being cheesy)

Write in my voice. Make it persuasive but not hype-y.
```

---

## 5. Email Nurture Sequence

```
Create a 5-email nurture sequence for people on my email list who haven't bought yet.

Email 1: Value bomb (give something useful)
Email 2: Story (how I solved this problem myself)
Email 3: Case study (client success, anonymized)
Email 4: Objection handler (address common concerns)
Email 5: Soft close (invitation to work together)

Each email should be 200-300 words. Include subject lines.
```

---

## 6. Workshop/ Webinar Outline

```
Create a complete workshop outline on [TOPIC]. Include:
- Title that gets people to register
- 3 learning objectives
- 45-minute content outline with timing
- 2 interactive exercises or polls
- 15-minute Q&A section
- Close with natural transition to my offer

Include speaker notes with exact wording for key sections.
```

---

## 7. Client Check-In Template

```
Write a weekly client check-in template I can reuse. Include:
- Wins from last week (celebrate progress)
- Current challenges (what's blocking them)
- Focus for this week (one priority)
- Accountability questions (am I doing what I said I'd do?)
- Space for them to ask for help

Make it warm but direct. 5-7 questions max.
```

---

## 8. Testimonial Request Email

```
Write a testimonial request email that doesn't feel awkward. Include:
- Warm opening (reference their progress)
- Why I'm asking (helping others understand the work)
- Specific questions to guide their testimonial:
  - What was the biggest challenge before working with me?
  - What was the biggest result or transformation?
  - What would you say to someone considering this programme?
- Easy way to submit (reply to email, record video, etc.)
- Gratitude

Make it easy for them to say yes.
```

## 9. Programme Curriculum Builder

```
Help me design a [NUMBER]-week coaching programme curriculum.

My clients struggle with: [LIST PAIN POINTS]
My clients want to achieve: [LIST DESIRED OUTCOMES]

Create a week-by-week curriculum that:
- Starts with mindset/foundation
- Builds to strategy and action
- Includes accountability mechanisms
- Ends with integration and next steps

For each week, include: Topic, Learning Objective, Action Step, Support Element.
```

## 10. Lead Magnet Creator

```
Create a complete lead magnet on [TOPIC]. Include:
- Title that promises a specific outcome
- Introduction (why this matters)
- 5-7 actionable tips or steps
- Examples or case studies for each
- Conclusion with invitation to work with me
- Call-to-action at the end

Format it so I can drop it into a PDF designer. 2-3 pages of content.
```
